The Basics: Quantum mechanic properties

Superposition is when a quantum particle is at different places, or in different states at the same time.

Quantum particles can become entangled allowing us to learn the position or state of particle B from particle A measurements.

The third Quantum mechanics property used in computing is Interference. Electromagnetic waves a generated at specific frequencies in-order to push qubits towards the position of interest. This works in the same way noise cancelling headphones produce the opposite wave to cancel out ambient noise.
